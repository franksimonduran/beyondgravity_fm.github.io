How AI Detection Works

Transit Method

The most common technique for detecting exoplanets is observing the tiny dips in a star's brightness when a planet passes in front of it.

AI analyzes these light curves to distinguish between real transits and false positives caused by other astronomical phenomena.

1

Data Collection

Space telescopes capture millions of stellar brightness measurements over time.

2

Preprocessing

Data is cleaned and normalized to remove noise and instrumental artifacts.

3

AI Analysis

Machine learning algorithms search for characteristic patterns of planetary transits.

4

Validation

The most promising candidates are verified with additional observations.

AI Techniques Used

Convolutional Neural Networks

Especially effective for identifying patterns in sequential data like stellar light curves.

Classification Algorithms

Distinguish between real transits and false positives with accuracy exceeding 96%.

Transfer Learning

Models trained with Kepler data are adapted to analyze information from new telescopes like TESS.

Notable Achievements

Kepler-90i

Discovered by Google AI in 2017, it was the first exoplanet found using neural networks applied to Kepler data.

TESS Exoplanets

AI has identified hundreds of exoplanet candidates in TESS data, significantly accelerating the discovery process.

Atmosphere Analysis

AI is also used to analyze exoplanet atmospheres, searching for biosignatures that could indicate habitability.
